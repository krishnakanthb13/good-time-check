## 🌌 The Core Ideology

The ideology of AuraTime is **"Empowered Scheduling."** 

In the high-speed modern world, we often rush into decisions without considering the energetic quality of time. Vedic astrology (Jyotisha) suggests that time is not just a linear measurement but a cyclical flow of different energies. AuraTime identifies three specific segments of the day where the cosmic "weather" is considered turbulent or obstructive.

By avoiding these short windows (approx. 90 minutes each), users can align their activities with more favorable flows, leading to reduced friction and smoother execution of projects.

## 🌑 The Three Time Ranges (The "Big Three")

Vedic astrology divides the daylight hours (sunrise to sunset) into 8 equal segments. Three of these segments carry specific historical and astrological weight.

### 1. Rahu Kalam (Shadow Planet Time)
*   **Significance**: Ruled by the shadow planet **Rahu**. In Vedic lore, Rahu is associated with illusion, chaos, and unexpected disruptions.
*   **Ideology**: This is a time of "mental fog." It is considered the most inauspicious time for initiating new ventures.
*   **Avoid**: Signing contracts, starting journeys, or launching new products.
*   **Safe To**: Continue tasks that were already started before the period began.

### 2. Yamagandam (Danger Time)
*   **Significance**: Associated with **Yama**, the deity of death and justice. It is often called the "Danger Time."
*   **Ideology**: If Rahu Kalam is about chaos, Yamagandam is about "finality" or "death of energy." 
*   **Avoid**: Important business meetings, weddings, or making major purchases. Anything started here is believed to face severe setbacks or fail entirely.
*   **Safe To**: Perform rituals or tasks related to closure or honoring the departed.

### 3. Gulika Kalam (The Repeater)
*   **Significance**: Linked to **Gulika**, the son of Saturn (Shani). 
*   **Ideology**: This period has a unique "Repeating Property." Whatever is initiated during Gulika Kalam is believed to happen again and again.
*   **Avoid**: Funerals or incurring debt (to prevent them from repeating).
*   **Auspicious For**: Some traditions suggest starting things you *want* to repeat—like meeting friends or performing charity. However, for most business tasks, it is treated as a period of obstacles.

## 📏 Calculation Logic (The 1/8th Rule)

AuraTime uses the standard Vedic 1/8th Segment Rule. 

1.  **Baseline**: Total daylight is calculated (Standard: 06:00 to 18:00 = 12 hours).
2.  **Segmentation**: 12 hours / 8 = **90-minute segments**.
3.  **Rotation**: Each day of the week, the positions of Rahu, Yama, and Gulika shift according to a fixed traditional order.

## 🛠️ Limitations vs. Utility

AuraTime is a **utility tool**, designed for instant-access "energetic weather" checks.

*   **Sunrise Variation**: We use a fixed 06:00 AM baseline for speed, zero-dependency performance, and absolute privacy. 
*   **Precision Trade-off**: While local sunrise varies, the standard table is used as a reliable baseline for general planning. 
*   **Future Roadmap**: To address the needs of high-precision practitioners, we are exploring an opt-in **"Ephemeris Mode"** or a separate **"AuraTime Pro"** tool for GPS-specific calculations.
*   **No Personal Charting**: AuraTime tracks the *general* quality of time available to everyone, not specific Janma Kundali.
